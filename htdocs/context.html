The Doha Agreement

On the 29th of February 2020, US and Taliban representatives came together in Doha to sign an agreement to end the ongoing conflict in Afghanistan. In this agreement, both parties agreed on the following terms:

  1. The US and other allied nations would gradually withdraw and reduce their stationed forces in the following months, with the expected completion date on the 1st of May 2021 (however, due to delay, the actual dates were on the final days of August).
  2. In return, the Taliban (formally known as the Islamic Emirate of Afghanistan) would not host any organizations or parties nor use its resources to pose a defense or security threat to the US and other allied nations.
  3. Finally, negotiations and ceasefire between the Islamic Republic of Afghanistan and the Taliban would follow after the withdrawal of said forces.
