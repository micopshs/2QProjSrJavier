The Doha Agreement

On the 29th of February 2020, US and Taliban representatives came together in Doha to sign an agreement to end the ongoing conflict in Afghanistan. In this agreement, both parties agreed on the following terms:

  1. The US and other allied nations would gradually withdraw and reduce their stationed forces in the following months, with the expected completion date on the 1st of May 2021 (however, due to delay, the actual dates were on the final days of August).
  2. In return, the Taliban (formally known as the Islamic Emirate of Afghanistan) would not host any organizations or parties nor use its resources to pose a defense or security threat to the US and other allied nations.
  3. Finally, negotiations and ceasefire between the Islamic Republic of Afghanistan and the Taliban would follow after the withdrawal of said forces (Agreement for Bringing Peace to Afghanistan, 2020).

The 2021 Taliban Offensive

In May 2021, while the Western troops were withdrawing, Taliban forces began attacking and capturing some of Afghanistan's districts. In some areas, the Taliban committed the war crime of harming the civilian population.

From June to July 2021, the Taliban continued their onslaught by capturing more districts, territories, and even cities from the government. At the same time, US and UK forces continued to withdraw from their major military outposts.

In August 2021, the Taliban won against the government forces. Their fighters captured territory after territory in the first weeks alone. Eventually, all that was left was to take the seat of power: Kabul. However, it did not take them long to capture the capital and threw everyone affected (e.g., government, foreign nations, citizens, etc.) in a panic.

With the capital, Kabul, captured, the evacuations now began (Brader, 2021).
